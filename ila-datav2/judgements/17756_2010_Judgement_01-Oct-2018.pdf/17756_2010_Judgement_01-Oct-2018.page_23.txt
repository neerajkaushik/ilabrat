“ The only exception to this rule is where the additional procedure though unauthorised , is necessary in order to save the life or preserve the health of the patient and it would be unreasonable to delay such unauthorised procedure until patient regains consciousness and takes a decision . ” 38 . In our opinion , the case of the appellant also falls in the excepted category mentioned by this Court because the appellant having noticed the abnormalities in the Gall Bladder while performing laparoscopy surgery proceeded to perform the conventional surgery and that too after obtaining fresh consent of respondent No.1 ’s husband . In other words , it was not an unauthorized act of the appellant and he could legally perform on the basis of original consent ( clause 4 ) of respondent No.1 as also on the basis of the further consent given by the respondent No . 1 ’s husband . 23
