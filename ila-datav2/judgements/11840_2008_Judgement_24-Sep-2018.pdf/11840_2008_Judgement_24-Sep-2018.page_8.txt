Substantial justice demands that a litigant is entitled for a right to be heard before any order is passed against him . ( See Sangram Singh vs. Election Tribunal , Kotah AIR 1955 SC 425 ) 13 ) In view of the forgoing discussion , the appeals succeed and are accordingly allowed . Impugned orders are set aside . The case is remanded to the Division Bench for deciding the writ appeal out of which these appeals arise afresh on merits in accordance with law . Needless to say , the appellantBank would be entitled to raise all pleas in their appeal before the High Court while prosecuting the appeal .
