That “ jurisdiction ” is a coat of many colours , and that the said word displays a certain colour depending upon the context in which it is mentioned , is well - settled . Tara Chand [ ( 1905 ) ILR 33 Cal 68 ] it was stated that jurisdiction may be defined to be the power of a Court to hear and determine a cause , to adjudicate and exercise any judicial power in relation to it : in other words , by jurisdiction is meant the authority which a Court has to decide matters that are litigated before it or to take cognizance of matters presented in a formal way for its decision .
