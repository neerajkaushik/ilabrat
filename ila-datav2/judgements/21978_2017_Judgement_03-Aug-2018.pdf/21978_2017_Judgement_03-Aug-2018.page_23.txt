In other words , once the possession of the acquired land is taken , the State has no power to withdraw from the acquisition because as a result of taking over of the possession , the acquired land vests with the State absolutely free from all encumbrances . 39 ) A fortiori so long as the possession is not taken of the acquired land , the State is at liberty to withdraw from the acquisition either partly or fully depending upon the facts of each case . 40 ) Section 16 of the Act empowers the Collector to take possession of the acquired land on passing of an award under Section 11 of the Act . Once the Collector takes possession , the acquired land vests absolutely in the Government free from all encumbrances as provided therein . 23
