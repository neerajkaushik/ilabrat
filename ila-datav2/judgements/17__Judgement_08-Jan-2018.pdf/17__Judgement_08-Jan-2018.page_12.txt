As per the said decision in para ( 38.5 ) , " In a case where the employee has made declaration truthfully of a concluded criminal case , the employer still has the right to consider antecedents , and can not be compelled to appoint the candidate . " 13 . It is thus well settled that acquittal in a criminal case does not automatically entitle him for appointment to the post . Still it is open to the employer to consider the antecedents and examine whether he is suitable for appointment to the post . From the observations of this Court in Mehar Singh and Parvez Khan cases , it is clear that a candidate to be recruited to the police service must be of impeccable character and integrity .
