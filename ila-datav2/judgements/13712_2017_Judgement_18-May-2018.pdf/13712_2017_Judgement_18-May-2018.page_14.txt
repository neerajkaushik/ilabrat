120.5 . The scope of preliminary inquiry is not to verify the veracity or otherwise of the information received but only to ascertain whether the information reveals any cognizable offence . 120.6 . As to what type and in which cases preliminary inquiry is to be conducted will depend on the facts and circumstances of each case . The category of cases in which preliminary inquiry may be made are as under : ( a ) Matrimonial disputes / family disputes ( b ) Commercial offences ( c ) Medical negligence cases ( da ) Corruption cases ( e ) Cases where there is abnormal delay / laches in initiating criminal prosecution , for example , over 3 months ' delay in reporting the matter without satisfactorily explaining the reasons for delay . The aforesaid are only illustrations and not exhaustive of all conditions which may warrant preliminary inquiry . 120.7 .
