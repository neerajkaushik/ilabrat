Such permissions must be granted for recorded reasons which must be served on the person to be arrested and to the concerned court . As and when a person arrested is produced before the Magistrate , the Magistrate must apply his mind to the reasons recorded and further detention should be allowed only if the reasons recorded are found to be valid . To avoid false implication , before FIR is registered , preliminary enquiry may be made whether the case falls in the parameters of the Atrocities Act and is not frivolous or motivated .
