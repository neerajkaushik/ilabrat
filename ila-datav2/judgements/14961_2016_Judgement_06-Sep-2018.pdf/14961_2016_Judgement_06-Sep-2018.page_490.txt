The mere fact that the LGBT persons constitute a “ miniscule fraction ” of the country ’s population can not be a ground to deprive them of their Fundamental Rights guaranteed by Part III of the Constitution . Even though the LGBT constitute a sexual minority , members of the LGBT community are citizens of this country who are equally entitled to the enforcement of their Fundamental Rights guaranteed by Articles 14 , 15 , 19 , and 21 . Fundamental Rights are guaranteed to all citizens alike , irrespective of whether they are a numerical minority . Modern democracies are based on the twin principles of majority rule , and protection of fundamental rights guaranteed under Part III of the Constitution .
