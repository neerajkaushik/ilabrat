Such alterable parts or practices are definitely not the ' core ' of religion where the belief is based and religion is founded upon . It could only be treated as mere embellishments to the non-essential part or practices . 125 . This view of ours is further substantiated by the fact that where a practice changes with the efflux of time , such a practice can not , in view of the law laid down in Commissioner of Police and others ( supra ) , be regarded as a core upon which a religion is formed . There has to be unhindered continuity in a practice for it to attain the status of essential practice . It is further discernible from the judgment of the High Court in S. Mahendran ( supra ) that the Devaswom Board had accepted before the High Court that female worshippers of the age group of 10 to 50 years used to visit the temple and conduced poojas in every month for five days for the first rice feeding ceremony of their children . The Devaswom Board also took a stand before the High Court that restriction of entry for women was only during Mandalam , Makaeavilakku and Vishnu days .
