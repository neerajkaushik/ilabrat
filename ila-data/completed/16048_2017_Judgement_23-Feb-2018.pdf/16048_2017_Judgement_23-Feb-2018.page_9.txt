Article 224 as originally contained in the Constitution did not work well and neither found adequate nor satisfactory . The Parliament to combat mounting arrears of the cases in the High Courts amended Article 224 by substituting existing Article 224 by a new Article providing for appointment of Additional Judges . Article 224 as amended by the Constitution ( Seventh Amendment ) Act , 1956 is as follows : “ Article 224 . Appointment of additional and acting Judges . - ( 1 ) . - If by reason of any temporary increase in the business of High Court or by reason of arrears of work therein , it appears to the President that the number of the Judges of that Court should be for the time being increased , the President may appoint duly qualified persons to be additional Judges of the Court for such period not exceeding two years as he may specify .
