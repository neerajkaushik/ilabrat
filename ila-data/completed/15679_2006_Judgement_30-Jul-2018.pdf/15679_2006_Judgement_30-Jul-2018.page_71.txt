But , in a situation where the tax exemption has to be interpreted , the benefit of doubt should go in favour of the revenue , the aforesaid conclusions are expounded only as a prelude to better understand jurisprudential basis for our conclusion . We may now consider the decisions which support our view . In Hansraj Gordhandas _ Case _ ( supra ) , the Constitutional Bench unanimously pointed out that an exemption from taxation is to be allowed based wholly by the language of the notification and exemption can not be gathered by necessary implication or by construction of words ; in other words , one has to look to the language alone and the object and purpose for granting exemption is irrelevant and immaterial .
